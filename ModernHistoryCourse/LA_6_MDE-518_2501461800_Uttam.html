Course Overview

This course explores major political, economic and social transformations in India (1757–1947). Designed for open & distance learners, content is delivered as printed SLM, downloadable PDFs, audio MP3s and short videos. The course follows an ACCESS-friendly design (mobile and low-bandwidth tolerant).

Weekly Modules (12 weeks)

  • Week 1: Introduction & Methods of History
  • Week 2: Mughal Decline & Regional States
  • Week 3: Company Rule & Economic Change
  • Week 4: Land Revenue Systems & Agrarian Change
  • Week 5: Social & Religious Reform Movements
  • Week 6: Colonial Economy & Migration
  • Week 7: Education, Print & Public Sphere
  • Week 8: Nationalism I (early movements)
  • Week 9: Nationalism II (20th century)
  • Week 10: Partition - causes & consequences
  • Week 11: Historiography & Interpretation
  • Week 12: Final synthesis & e-portfolio

Resources

All core materials are distributed as printed SLM and downloadable documents. Put the files into the folders shown so NVU can link to them locally.

Assessment

  • Weekly quizzes & participation: 20%
  • Three short source-analysis assignments: 30%
  • Group mini-project (oral history/local study): 25%
  • Final e-portfolio / term-end exam: 25%

Tutor & Student Support

Regional tutors (25–40 students each) provide feedback via LMS and WhatsApp. Study centres distribute printed SLMs and host face-to-face exam centres.
